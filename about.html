What drives me

The pattern I kept seeing, and why I do what I do.

I kept running into the same pattern: businesses held together by spreadsheets, WhatsApp messages, and sticky notes. Bookings getting lost, payments tracked in notebooks, customer data scattered across five different apps. Nobody was happy with the chaos, but off-the-shelf software never quite fit the way they actually worked.

So I learned to build what actually fits. I taught myself to design and code systems that match how a business actually works — not the other way around. Every project starts with a single question: "What's actually broken?" Everything else follows from that answer.

I'm early in taking on client work, which means every project gets my full attention — no distractions, no juggling multiple clients, no outsourcing. Your problem becomes my problem until it's solved.
